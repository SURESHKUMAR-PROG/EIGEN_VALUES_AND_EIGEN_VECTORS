Aim

The primary goal of this experiment is to compute and visualize the Eigen-structure (Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors) of a square matrix and verify fundamental algebraic properties.

Learning Objectives:

  • Understand the geometric meaning of Eigen-transformations.
  • Master the derivation of the Characteristic Equation.
  • Learn the numerical method to find basis vectors for eigenspaces.
  • Verify properties relating Trace/Determinant to Eigenvalues.

Theory

For a square matrix A, a scalar value lambda is an eigenvalue if there exists a non-zero vector x, known as the eigenvector, such that:

A times x = lambda times x

This equation indicates that the transformation A acts on vector x simply by scaling its magnitude by a factor of lambda without changing its direction.

The Characteristic Equation

Rearranging the equation to (A minus lambda times I) times x = 0, where I is the identity matrix. For a non-trivial solution (where vector x is not zero), the matrix (A minus lambda times I) must be non-invertible:

Determinant of (A - lambda times I) = 0

Key Properties

  • Sum Property: The sum of all eigenvalues equals the Trace of the matrix.
  • Product Property: The product of all eigenvalues equals the Determinant of the matrix.
  • Independence: Eigenvectors corresponding to distinct eigenvalues are linearly independent of one another.

Detailed Procedure

The following methodology is used to determine the eigen-structure of a given square matrix A.

Step 1: Form the Characteristic Matrix

Define the matrix A and identify the Identity matrix I of the same dimension. Construct the characteristic matrix by subtracting the variable lambda from the main diagonal elements of matrix A.

Equation: Characteristic Matrix = A - (lambda times I)

Step 2: Obtain the Characteristic Polynomial

Calculate the determinant of the characteristic matrix found in Step 1. Equating this determinant to zero provides the characteristic equation.

General Form for 2 by 2: lambda squared - (Trace of A) times lambda + (Determinant of A) = 0

Step 3: Calculate Eigenvalues

Solve the polynomial equation for lambda. The number of solutions will match the dimension of the matrix. These solutions represent the scalar factors of the transformation.

Step 4: Solve for Eigenvectors

For each unique eigenvalue, substitute its value into the system of homogeneous equations. Use row reduction or Gaussian elimination to find the non-zero solutions for the vector components.

Equation System: (A - eigenvalue times I) times [vector components] = [zero vector]

Step 5: Verification of Results

Confirm the accuracy by checking that the sum of the derived eigenvalues matches the trace of the original matrix, and their product equals the matrix determinant.

Real-world Applications

Data Science

PCA: Principal Component Analysis uses eigenvectors of a covariance matrix to identify patterns in high-dimensional data.

Web Ranking

Google PageRank: The importance of a webpage is calculated as the dominant eigenvector of a link matrix.

Structural Engineering

Bridges and skyscrapers have "natural frequencies" which are the eigenvalues of their mass and stiffness matrices. Engineers ensure wind forces don't match these values to prevent collapse.
